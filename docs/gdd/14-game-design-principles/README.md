# Game Design Principles & Emergent Play

## Core Design Pillars

### 1. Everything Is Systems, Not Scripts

No event is hard-coded to produce a specific outcome. Every event is the RESULT of systems interacting. The DM narrates what the systems produce — it doesn't override them.

A scandal isn't "scripted drama." It's:
- An NPC with low loyalty + high knowledge of your secret
- A journalist NPC with a relationship to that NPC
- The journalist's ambition exceeding their fear of you
- The media org's editorial leaning amplifying or burying it
- Your press secretary's competence determining response time
- Your existing approval determining how much damage it does

### 2. Consequences Are Real and Permanent

No take-backs. No forgotten sins. The social graph has memory. The economy has inertia. Laws persist until repealed.

- Short-term wins can be long-term disasters
- Burning a bridge week 5 might cost you a vote week 40
- A law you passed carelessly can backfire later
- Your reputation is the sum of ALL your actions

### 3. Every Playthrough Is a Unique Story

Replayability from: different archetypes × starting positions × eras, procedural NPC personalities, random event seeding, player-driven custom events, dice variance, economic sensitivity, social graph dynamics, meta-progression unlocks.

Same archetype, same era, same start = different story every time.

### 4. Depth Without Complexity Walls

New player plays in 5 minutes. Chat-forward UI means: read, type, play.

Depth reveals through play:
- Week 1: "I want to talk to people and pass laws"
- Week 10: "I need to manage my deck for debate power"
- Week 30: "I'm running alliances across three government levels during an economic crisis"
- Week 50: "The law I passed in week 12 is why the Supreme Court ruled against me"

### 5. The AI Is a Dungeon Master, Not a Game Designer

Gemma 4 narrates and adjudicates. Systems decide what happens. The DM decides how to tell the story.

### 6. Failure Is Content

Losing an election is a pivot point, not game over. Getting impeached is a dramatic arc. A failed assassination attempt is the most interesting thing that can happen.

Meta-progression reinforces: you earn legacy points from ALL endings. Some unlocks REQUIRE failure.

## Emergent Scenarios

These should be POSSIBLE but never SCRIPTED:

| Scenario | How It Emerges |
|----------|---------------|
| **The Kingmaker** | You never run for president but your network picks who does |
| **The Reformer** | Constitutional amendment changes how government works in-game |
| **The Survivor** | Three scandals, impeachment attempt, and you're still standing via debts |
| **The Dynasty Builder** | Retire as governor, son runs for congress using your network |
| **The Revolutionary** | Never hold office but reshape politics through grassroots + media |
| **The Corrupt Operator** | Control officeholders from the shadows via bribes and blackmail |
| **The Accidental President** | Everyone above self-destructed, you're last one standing |
| **The Bipartisan Bridge** | Balanced positions, called flip-flopper but build impossible coalitions |

## Roguelike DNA

| Roguelike Concept | POLIT Translation |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| Permadeath | Career death — your career can end permanently |
| Procedural generation | Procedural politics — NPCs, events, economy vary per run |
| Run-based learning | Political education — each run teaches how systems work |
| Item synergy | Card synergy — three card types create combo potential |
| Risk/reward escalation | Political ambition — higher office = more power + more scrutiny |
| Seeds | Scenario configuration — shareable starting conditions |
| Daily challenge | Weekly scenario — same start, compare legacy scores |

## Anti-Stagnation Systems

### Escalating Opposition
- Media scrutiny scales with office level
- NPC ambition activates — allies want YOUR job
- International crises increase in frequency
- Economic cycles prevent permanent prosperity
- Term limits force career transitions

### Generational Shift
- NPCs age, retire, get replaced
- Voter demographics shift
- Issues that defined your rise become obsolete
- New movements challenge your positions
- Position cards can become "outdated" as Overton Window shifts

### Legacy Pressure
- After peak office, focus shifts to cementing changes
- Court appointments, amendments, institutional changes are endgame
- Can you cement your legacy before the next generation undoes it?

### Health and Age
- Characters age. Health degrades with stress and time.
- 75-year-old president has fewer AP and higher health risk.
- Natural run endpoints without forcing them.

### Chaos Events
- Low-probability, high-impact events (pandemic, assassination attempt, constitutional crisis)
- Frequency increases slightly in longer runs
- Prevents comfortable equilibrium
